
This project analyzes direct marketing campaign data from a banking institution to identify the key factors influencing term deposit subscriptions. The goal is to transform raw data into actionable insights to optimize future targeting strategies and improve conversion rates.


# Used skills:
*Language:* SQL (Window Functions, CTEs, Aggregated Filtering)

*Skills:* Data Loading (COPY command), Data Cleaning, Feature Engineering


# Key Insights & Analysis:


***1. Demographic & Social Profiling***
**Age Groups:**

I segmented customers using CASE WHEN logic to identify which age brackets are most responsive to the campaign.

**Education Level:**

    Analyzed how academic background influences the likelihood of subscribing to a financial product.

**Job Types:**

    Identified specific professions with the highest conversion rates to refine customer personas.



***2. Financial Factors & Risk Assessment***
**Account Balance:**

    Compared subscription rates between "High" and "Low" balance customers using CTAs and subqueries.

**Risk Indicators:**

    Evaluated the impact of credit defaults and existing loans (housing/personal) on a customer's decision to subscribe.



***3. Operational Campaign Efficiency***
**Communication Channels:**

    Determined the most effective contact method (e.g., Cellular vs. Telephone).

**Call Duration:**

    Analyzed the correlation between talk time and successful conversions to optimize call scripts.

**Seasonality:**

Identified peak subscription months to better allocate marketing resources throughout the year.
